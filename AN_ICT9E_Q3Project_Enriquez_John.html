Our website provides safety regulations and awareness to fire incidents.

Never leave your kitchen when cooking

Make sure to keep the heat on low. And if you need to leave the kitchen for any reason, make sure to have someone watch over the food.

Inspect your heating sources

Your home's heating sources could very easily start a house fire if they're not functioning correctly. Always change the filters in your air conditioner, maintain your heaters away from anything that might burst into flames, and have a technician inspect these things once a year.

Store any flammable products correctly

When exposed to a severe source of heat, household cleansers and chemicals like strong hold hairspray or creams may become somewhat hazardous. Always store these safely locked up and away from heaters in a cool location.

Maintain your wires

Try to be courteous and inspect the wire for breakage or chewed before connecting anything into a socket (in case you have any pets running around). Damaged wires must be replaced as soon as possible as they may provide a hazard for a fire. Furthermore, keep in mind that when in use, wires could get hot, so pay more attention to where they lay them and avoid passing them through carpets or other potentially flammable materials.

Be cautious with open flames

Prevent opening candles that may cause further hazard to the surroundings that may trigger a fire.

Keep a fire extinguisher at all times

Your best last resort in the case of a fire beginning will be blankets and fire extinguishers. To ensure that whatever does burst into flames in your home can be extinguished as soon as possible, every member of the family should be knowledgeable with how to use a fire extinguisher. Fire blankets are also useful for extinguishing little fires before they get larger and more furious.
