Twenty-Five, Twenty-One

Sypnosis

Nam Joo-Hyuk plays Baek Yi-Jin, a once wealthy son who lost everything, in the touching South Korean drama "Twenty Five Twenty One," whereas Kim Tae-Ri plays Na Hee-Do, a young aspirant fencer.

The late 1990s are depicted in flashbacks when Kim Min-Chae (Myung-Bin Choi), Na Hee-Do's daughter,quits ballet and goes to her grandmother's house. She later discovers her mother's diary.

After her school's fencing team was disbanded as a byproduct of the 1997 Asian financial crisis, Na, a once-prodigious fencer, now battles to convince herself and others that she can realize her full potential.

Na sets out on a journey to convince her mother and coach, who have been pressuring her to discontinue fencing. Her eccentric persona, however, is what hinders the urge to continue and fight.

Na Hee-Do is a member of her high school fencing team. Due to the South Korean financial crisis, the high school fencing team gets disbanded. Getting through all the difficulties, she becomes a member of the sabre fencing national team.

The oldest child of a major company was Baek Yi Jin. He was intelligent and had enrolled in a prestigious engineering program. Nevertheless, after the financial crisis, his family filed for bankruptcy, forcing his once-rich son to live apart from them and deliver newspapers door to door.

At the age of 17, Ko Yu Rim won the fencing championship and gained recognition in Korea. Notwithstanding her family's poor financial position, Yi Jin was sponsored by her father's employer. Economic stress followed their bankruptcy for Yu Rim and her family as well. They weren't able to support their daughter's expensive sport. For Yu Rim, fencing was no more just a passion; it was a way of subsistence. She had spent her entire life only fencing, so she had to work hard to make it through.

The most popular student in the university was Moon Ji Woong. He didn't give his studies any thought because he was passionate about fashion. Because of his unpredictable behavior, he frequently had problems with his professors at school. Even his mother thought badly of him and was constantly worried for his future.

JI Seung Wan excelled in school. She was also the director of the broadcasting club and the class monitor. Despite her knowledge and assistance to teachers, she was kind to every student in the class. She had become a trustworthy and well-liked class monitor as a consequence. She always studied, but she also owned a Wanseung private radio station. She discussed social concerns and issues that affect young people in her broadcasts. It was clear that she would continue to lead a very successful life.
